Guide for New Clients, Photographers and Artists

There are plenty of photography resources available online but not all are applicable to everyone. Rather than repeating the existing content, we attempt to add our flavor of expertise into the photography universe. Please note that the following recommendations are narrated based on our work experience in the photography industry. We hope you would find our insights helpful. If you do, please connect with us on social media to show us your support. The following recommendations are geared toward new clients and aspiring photographers to make wise decisions and to avoid potential pitfalls. And, we would greatly appreciate your constructive feedbacks.

Client: Hiring a Photographer

There are many options for clients when it comes to hiring a photographer. However, everyone market at different rate with variety of services. That makes it harder to find an appropriate photographer for the assignment. Meanwhile, measuring skills of one photographer with another is not practical and that is not the ideal strategy either. Like any other profession, photography takes time to master. Secondly, professional equipment is costly to own and maintain on top of studio overhead expenses. Meanwhile, post processing technologies have advanced over the years but image processing work require expertise skills to deliver consistent results. Pros do not get a perfect shot by chance; they create it under critical circumstances. On the flip-side, average consumers should not be paying thousands of dollars to document a simple event.

Tip: If you happen to have your event or photoshoot fall in the slow season, do not hesitate negotiate the rate with the photographer. Most freelance photographers will be able to offer some discounts depending on the circumstances.

At Views by Viji, we strive to strike the right balance between professionalism and value. That is why we encourage all of our potential clients to meet us for a coffee or tea to evaluate our work. Ask us to show you the sample prints. We will be happy to do that for you. Also, you get to know our personalities and creative styles to see whether that would be your interest. At the same time, if we feel that we may not deliver justice to your hard-earned dollars, we will certainly recommend one of our colleagues who can fulfil such requirements.

Photographer: Preparing for the Assignment

Prepare, practice and test shoot before every session. Remember failing to plan is simply planning to fail. Know your gear and its limitations. Never be afraid to take the baby step since everyone has to take it at some point anyways. Oddly, we find most amazing photographers are too humble at heart.

  • Connect with Your Subject

    know your subject and make them at ease; that is the key to a great shot. Authentic expression will bring life to any portrait.

  • As an Artist, Lead by Example

    Since you’re the artist and clients are the subject, just be polite and courteous even when they fail to corporate with you. Innocently, that may happen time to time.

  • Just Make it Happen

    Most often journalists do not have the opportunity to frame the perfect shot or dial the correct exposure. It the subject and the story that matters.

  • Creatively Solve Problems

    Always choose appropriate lens for the session as well as subject presentation. Try to de-emphasize any flaws artistically in camera if possible.

  • Be Optimistic at All Times

    Perfect background is not always possible, just work with your available options and be creative as you can. Be assured that not all days are rainbows and sun shines.

  • Utilize the Power of Nature

    Take advantage of natural light by analyzing light direction, amount and quality of light as well as any reflections that brings to the subject.

  • Place Yourself in Shoes of the Subject

    Most often flash makes subjects uncomfortable but it is a requirement depending on the circumstances. Use just enough amount of flash as needed for exposure. Also that would help you protect the skin tones.

  • Manage the Unnatural Shadows

    Avoid shadows under eyes, necks and clothes particularly in group shots where it is prone to happen when using flash. Do not hesitate to move subjects or light source if practical.

  • Always Protect the highlights

    Always protect the highlights since that is difficult to recover in post while managing the shadows, perfect exposure is always subjective to the scene.

  • Experintment With Exposure in Post and Print

    Shoot for the highlights and print for the shadows - well established practice. Only extensive experimentation and practice can help to you find your sweet spot.

  • Think Outside the Box

    Avoid common point shoot perspectives to add interest to your photography, clients will be thankful and appreciate your creative efforts.

  • Smiling is Not Everything

    Remember that smiling is one of the many expressions, depending on the client and the assignment, try to be creative as possible.

  • Be Cautious with Social Media

    Positive feedback is encouranging but negative comments can be hurtful. For a beginners, it is best to start slow within your close circles.

      • Be patient with yourself, you will get there eventually. Keeping practicing, do not compare yourself to anyone. Remember, thousand miles of journey start with a single step. Keep pushing the wheel, find inspirations and photographers that you feel would help you achieve more in your work. We look up to Sean Tucker, a popular youtuber, photographer and film maker based in London, United Kingdom. He is not just a great artist but a finest human being who crossed our minds. Particularly, his film on “Protect Your Highlights: A Lesson for Light and Life” a heart melting motivator for every aspiring photographer.

      Creativity: Industry Standards

      Being a photographer is just what appears on the surface but indeed post production is what makes a photographer stand out in the crowd and identifies as an artist. Beyond doubt, Adobe is the industry standard for creative professionals but there are more options. Regardless of the tool, just master the art. It is a long process but you will not be disappointed if artistry is your thing. If schooling is not practical, try to study online, plenty of artists and materials available online. Of course, Google is your friend.

      There to too many tutorials but all focuses on adding or changing colors, indeed, in professional photography, it is the opposite. The key is to get it right in camera and then use subtraction method to clean up the image before adding any creative colors or filters. Majority of the time spent in appropriate color balancing for the scene. Refrain from touching saturation, vibration or any color altering techniques till bringing the photo to appropriate exposure, highlight and shadow levels.

      Whenever practical, shoot and process photos with color theory in mind and crop appropriately to make the subject stand out. Use complementary colors in opposite spectrum of the color wheel. Make photos stand out through revealing the mood, environment and situations. The purpose of entire color work is to mimic the natural environment with little artistic touch for pleasing look. In post, we always strive for authentic expressions and realistic sceneries.
